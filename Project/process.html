Pre-Process Considerations

Items needed

  • Air Compressor (w/ hose, etc.)
  • RV Blowout Plug
  • Water Heater Tank Rinser (wand)
  • Tongue-and-Groove Pliers
  • Antifreeze

RV type considerations

There are many, many RV types. This means there is no one, exact winterizing
method for all types. The instructions are very general and intended to apply to
most types. If you are unsure about your particular set-up, feel free to send us a
message in the contact section.

Safety Guidelines

  • Always wear protective gear--eye protection and hand protection
  • Always let your lines and water heater cool before draining lines
  • Always empty your black and gray tank at an approved disposal station
  • Always relieve the pressure on your water heater before removing the plug

The Winterizing Process

Shut-off water, power and heat

  1. Disconnect hose from city water connection if applicable
  2. Turn off water pump
  3. Turn off water heater
  4. Wait until water is cooled

Drain Tanks

  1. Drain black tank (at approved dump station)
  2. Drain gray tank (at approved dump station)
  3. Drain fresh water tank by removing plug underneath RV

Bypass and drain the water heater

  1. Relieve the pressure in the water heater by opening/pulling the t/p valve
  2. Remove the water heater plug/anode with a wrench
  3. Let all the water drain out
  4. Set water heater valves to Bypass or winterize (located behind water heater)
  5. Replace plug/anode (mice can get in here over winter if left open)

Relieve pressure and begin draining system

  1. Locate hot and cold low point drains under RV and open them
  2. Inside RV open each faucet (hot and cold) to help drain water
  3. When draining stops, close all faucets
  4. Keep low point drains open

Attach air compressor

  1. Plug in and turn on air compressor
  2. Wait until compressor is full
  3. Set regulator to 30-40 PSI (setting the regular to high, can damage plumbing system)
  4. Screw the blowout plug into the city water inlet
  5. Connect on end of hose to compressor and the other end to the blowout plug

Drain each fixture

  1. Start with fixture furthest from the inlet (usually the kitchen sink)
  2. Open the COLD until only air comes out, then close and open HOT until only air comes out
  3. Once only air is coming out of both COLD and HOT close the fauct/fixture
  4. Repeat for each fixture, moving towards the inlet
  5. Hold the toilet flush lever until the the toilet supply line only blows clear air
  6. Turn off compressor, disconnect hose, and unscrew blowout plug

Protect traps and seals

  1. Pour 1-2 cups of antifreeze in each sink to protect the p-traps
  2. Add 1 cup of antifreeze into the toilet bowls and slowly flush to protect valve and seal

The De-Winterizing Process

Flush the water heater of any bacteria

  1. Keep valves bypassed
  2. Open plug/anode with wrench
  3. Thoroughly rinse water heater cavity by inserting water heater tank rinser into cavity
  4. If anode is heavily coroded replace it
  5. Reappy plumbers tape
  6. Close plug/anode tightly

Flush the fresh water tank of any bacteria

  1. Make sure plug is removed
  2. Fill tank with fresh water for about 10 minutes
  3. Once tank is drained, screw the plug back on

Bring system back on line

  1. Make sure all plugs (fresh water, low point drain) and fixtures are closed
  2. Fill the fresh water tank
  3. Open water heater bypass/winterize valves
  4. Turn on water pump to refill system
  5. Turn on water heater
  6. For each fixutre open COLD and HOT until air is removed from line
  7. Thoroughly flush the drain at each fixutre with enough water to rinse out antifreeze
